# Common Social Interaction Phrases

---
## おはようございます

**Reading:** おはようございます
**Meaning:** good morning (polite)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #greeting, #polite, #essential

### Example Sentences
- おはようございます、田中さん - Good morning, Tanaka-san
- おはよう (casual to friends/family) - Morning

### Notes
Used until about 10-11 AM. Drop ございます for casual situations with friends and family.

---

## こんにちは

**Reading:** こんにちは
**Meaning:** hello, good afternoon
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #greeting, #common, #essential

### Example Sentences
- こんにちは、元気ですか - Hello, how are you?
- こんにちは、先生 - Hello, teacher

### Notes
Most common greeting for daytime. Used from late morning through evening. Literally means "as for today..."

---

## こんばんは

**Reading:** こんばんは
**Meaning:** good evening
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #greeting, #polite

### Example Sentences
- こんばんは、お母さん - Good evening, mother
- こんばんは、皆さん（みなさん） - Good evening, everyone

### Notes
Used from evening onwards. Note: ends with は not わ despite pronunciation.

---

## おやすみなさい

**Reading:** おやすみなさい
**Meaning:** good night
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #greeting

### Example Sentences
- おやすみなさい、また明日（あした） - Good night, see you tomorrow
- おやすみ (casual) - Night

### Notes
Used when going to bed or saying goodbye late at night. Can shorten to おやすみ casually.

---

## さようなら

**Reading:** さようなら
**Meaning:** goodbye, farewell
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #parting, #formal

### Example Sentences
- さようなら、またね - Goodbye, see you again
- さようなら、皆さん - Goodbye, everyone

### Notes
Formal goodbye. Often implies you won't see the person for a while. More common: じゃあね or またね.

---

## じゃあね

**Reading:** じゃあね
**Meaning:** see you, bye (casual)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #parting, #casual, #common

### Example Sentences
- じゃあね、また明日 - See you, until tomorrow
- じゃ、またね - Well, see you later

### Notes
Very common casual goodbye. Also: じゃあまた, じゃね. Much more common than さようなら.

---

## またね

**Reading:** またね
**Meaning:** see you later
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #parting, #casual

### Example Sentences
- またね、楽しみにしてる - See you later, looking forward to it
- またね、気をつけて - See you later, take care

### Notes
Casual parting phrase. Literally "again, right?" Implies you'll meet again soon.

---

## ありがとう

**Reading:** ありがとう
**Meaning:** thank you
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #gratitude, #common, #essential

### Example Sentences
- ありがとう、助かった - Thank you, you helped me
- どうもありがとう - Thank you very much

### Notes
Casual thank you. Add ございます for politeness: ありがとうございます.

---

## ありがとうございます

**Reading:** ありがとうございます
**Meaning:** thank you (polite)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #gratitude, #polite, #essential

### Example Sentences
- ありがとうございます、助かりました - Thank you, you helped me (polite)
- どうもありがとうございます - Thank you very much

### Notes
Polite form of ありがとう. Use with strangers, seniors, customers, teachers.

---

## どうもありがとうございます

**Reading:** どうもありがとうございます
**Meaning:** thank you very much
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #gratitude, #polite

### Example Sentences
- どうもありがとうございます、本当に助かりました - Thank you very much, you really helped me

### Notes
Most polite thank you. どうも adds emphasis. Can also just say どうも casually.

---

## どういたしまして

**Reading:** どういたしまして
**Meaning:** you're welcome
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #polite

### Example Sentences
- A: ありがとうございます B: どういたしまして - A: Thank you B: You're welcome

### Notes
Polite response to thanks. Casual alternatives: いいえ, いえいえ, とんでもない.

---

## すみません

**Reading:** すみません
**Meaning:** excuse me, sorry, thank you
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #apology, #polite, #versatile, #essential

### Example Sentences
- すみません、トイレはどこですか - Excuse me, where is the toilet?
- すみません、遅れました - Sorry, I'm late
- (After receiving help) すみません - Thank you

### Notes
Very versatile: apologize, get attention, or thank (with humility). Essential phrase!

---

## ごめんなさい

**Reading:** ごめんなさい
**Meaning:** I'm sorry
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #apology, #polite

### Example Sentences
- ごめんなさい、間違えました - I'm sorry, I made a mistake
- 本当にごめんなさい - I'm really sorry

### Notes
More clearly an apology than すみません. Casual form: ごめん.

---

## ごめん

**Reading:** ごめん
**Meaning:** sorry (casual)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #apology, #casual

### Example Sentences
- ごめん、遅れた - Sorry, I'm late
- ごめん、ごめん - Sorry, sorry

### Notes
Casual apology for friends and family. Don't use with superiors or strangers.

---

## いただきます

**Reading:** いただきます
**Meaning:** expression before eating (I humbly receive)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #meal, #culture, #essential

### Example Sentences
- (Before eating) いただきます
- 食べる前にいただきますと言う - Say "itadakimasu" before eating

### Notes
Essential cultural phrase said before every meal. Shows gratitude for the food and those who prepared it.

---

## ごちそうさまでした

**Meaning:** thank you for the meal (after eating)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #meal, #culture, #essential

### Example Sentences
- (After eating) ごちそうさまでした

## よろしくお願いします

**Reading:** よろしくおねがいします
**Meaning:** nice to meet you, please treat me well
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #introduction, #polite, #essential

### Example Sentences
- 初めまして、山田です。よろしくお願いします - Nice to meet you, I'm Yamada
- 今日からよろしくお願いします - Please treat me well from today

### Notes
Versatile phrase for introductions and beginning relationships. Very important in Japanese culture.

---

## 初めまして

**Reading:** はじめまして
**Meaning:** how do you do, nice to meet you (first time)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #introduction, #polite

### Example Sentences
- 初めまして、田中と申します（もうします） - How do you do, I'm called Tanaka
- 初めまして、よろしくお願いします - Nice to meet you, pleased to meet you

### Notes
Used only at first meeting. Literally "for the first time". Always followed by よろしくお願いします.

## お元気ですか

**Reading:** おげんきですか
**Meaning:** how are you?
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #greeting, #polite

### Example Sentences
- こんにちは、お元気ですか - Hello, how are you?
- お久しぶりです。お元気ですか - Long time no see. How are you?

### Notes
Polite way to ask about someone's health/wellbeing. Casual: 元気？

## 元気です

**Reading:** げんきです
**Meaning:** I'm fine, I'm well
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- A: お元気ですか B: はい、元気です - A: How are you? B: Yes, I'm well
- 元気ですよ、ありがとう - I'm fine, thanks

### Notes
Standard response to お元気ですか. Can add ありがとうございます to be more polite.

---

## お久しぶりです

**Reading:** おひさしぶりです
**Meaning:** long time no see
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #greeting, #polite

### Example Sentences
- お久しぶりです、お元気でしたか - Long time no see, how have you been?
- 本当にお久しぶりですね - It's really been a while

### Notes
Used when you haven't seen someone for a long time. Casual: 久しぶり.

---

## いってきます

**Reading:** いってきます
**Meaning:** I'm leaving (lit: I'll go and come back)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #parting, #home, #culture

### Example Sentences
- (Leaving home) いってきます
- じゃあ、いってきます - Well, I'm off

### Notes
Said when leaving home. Shows you intend to return. Family responds: いってらっしゃい.

---

## いってらっしゃい

**Reading:** いってらっしゃい
**Meaning:** have a good day, see you later (response to いってきます)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #home, #culture

### Example Sentences
- A: いってきます B: いってらっしゃい - A: I'm leaving B: Have a good day

### Notes
Response when someone leaves home. Shows you expect them back. Family/household phrase.

---

## ただいま

**Reading:** ただいま
**Meaning:** I'm home, I'm back
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #arrival, #home, #culture

### Example Sentences
- (Arriving home) ただいま
- ただいま、疲れた - I'm home, I'm tired

### Notes
Said when returning home. Literally "just now". Family responds: おかえり(なさい).

---

## おかえり(なさい)

**Reading:** おかえり(なさい)
**Meaning:** welcome home (response to ただいま)
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #home, #culture

### Example Sentences
- A: ただいま B: おかえり - A: I'm home B: Welcome back
- おかえりなさい、お疲れ様 - Welcome home, you must be tired

### Notes
Response when someone comes home. おかえり is casual, おかえりなさい is polite.

**Meaning:** excuse me for leaving first

### Example Sentences

## 大丈夫です

**Reading:** だいじょうぶです
**Meaning:** it's okay, I'm fine, no problem
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- A: すみません B: 大丈夫です - A: Sorry B: It's okay
- 大丈夫ですか - Are you okay?
- はい、大丈夫です - Yes, I'm fine

### Notes
Very versatile phrase. Can mean "it's okay", "I'm fine", "no problem", "are you okay?".

---

## いいえ

**Reading:** いいえ
**Meaning:** no
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #essential

### Example Sentences
- A: 日本人ですか B: いいえ、違います - A: Are you Japanese? B: No, I'm not
- いいえ、結構です（けっこうです） - No, I'm fine (polite refusal)

### Notes
Polite "no". Casual: ううん. Also used to say "you're welcome" or deny thanks.

---

## はい

**Reading:** はい
**Meaning:** yes
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #essential

### Example Sentences
- A: 学生ですか B: はい、そうです - A: Are you a student? B: Yes, I am
- はい、分かりました - Yes, I understand

### Notes
Polite "yes". Also used to acknowledge you're listening or to respond to your name being called.

---

## そうです

**Reading:** そうです
**Meaning:** that's right, yes
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- A: これは本ですか B: はい、そうです - A: Is this a book? B: Yes, it is
- そうですね - That's right (with agreement)

### Notes
Confirms or agrees with a statement. そうですか means "I see" or "is that so?".

---

## そうですか

**Reading:** そうですか
**Meaning:** I see, is that so?
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- A: 明日休みです B: そうですか - A: Tomorrow is a holiday B: I see
- そうですか、知りませんでした - I see, I didn't know

### Notes
Shows you received new information. Rising intonation = question, falling = acknowledgment.

---

## 分かりました

**Reading:** わかりました
**Meaning:** I understand, I got it
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- A: 明日9時に来てください B: はい、分かりました - A: Please come at 9 tomorrow B: Yes, understood
- 分かりました、そうします - I understand, I'll do that

### Notes
Shows understanding of information or instructions. Casual: 分かった.

**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5

## 失礼します

**Reading:** しつれいします
**Meaning:** excuse me (entering/leaving), I'm sorry to bother you
**Type:** phrase
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #polite, #formal

### Example Sentences
- (Entering a room) 失礼します - Excuse me
- (Leaving) では、失礼します - Well then, excuse me
- 失礼ですが、お名前は？ - Excuse me, but what's your name?

### Notes
Very polite phrase for entering/leaving rooms or when asking personal questions. Shows respect.

## お邪魔します

**Meaning:** thank you for having me (leaving someone's home)

## 頑張って(ください)

**Meaning:** good luck, do your best

**Level:** N5
