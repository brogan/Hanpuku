# JLPT N5 Counters (助数詞)
# 
# ABOUT THIS SET:
# Japanese counters are special suffixes attached to numbers when counting objects.
# Different counters are used depending on the shape, size, or type of object being counted.
# This set covers the most essential N5-level counters.
#
# USAGE PATTERN: [number] + [counter]
# Example: 三人 (さんにん) = three people
#
# Note: Many counters cause phonetic changes to numbers (rendaku/euphonic changes).
# These changes are noted in each entry.

---
## 人

**Reading:** にん / り
**Meaning:** counter for people
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #counter, #people, #common

### Example Sentences
- 一人 (ひとり) - one person
- 二人 (ふたり) - two people  
- 三人 (さんにん) - three people
- 何人 (なんにん) - how many people

### Notes
Irregular readings for 1 and 2: 一人 (ひとり), 二人 (ふたり). From 3 onwards uses regular pattern: 三人 (さんにん), 四人 (よにん), etc. Question word: 何人 (なんにん). Most common and essential counter at N5 level.

## つ

**Reading:** つ
**Meaning:** general counter for objects
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #counter, #general, #common

### Example Sentences
- 一つ (ひとつ) - one (thing)
- 二つ (ふたつ) - two (things)
- 三つ (みっつ) - three (things)
- いくつ - how many

### Notes
Native Japanese counter, only goes up to 10: ひとつ、ふたつ、みっつ、よっつ、いつつ、むっつ、ななつ、やっつ、ここのつ、とお. Used for general objects when you don't know the specific counter, or for abstract things like ideas. Question word: いくつ (how many/how old).

**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #counter, #floors, #common

### Example Sentences
- 一階 (いっかい) - first floor
- 二階 (にかい) - second floor
- 三階 (さんがい) - third floor

Used for: building floors. Phonetic changes: 3 uses がい (さんがい). 1 is いっかい. Question word: 何階 (なんがい or なんかい - both acceptable). Note: In Japan, 一階 is ground floor (like UK system), not second floor (like US system).

## 歳 / 才

**Reading:** さい
**Meaning:** counter for age
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #counter, #age, #common

### Example Sentences
- 一歳 (いっさい) - one year old
- 二十歳 (はたち) - twenty years old
- 三十歳 (さんじゅっさい) - thirty years old
- 何歳 (なんさい) - how old

### Notes
Used for: age of people and animals. Irregular: 二十歳 is はたち (not にじゅっさい). 1,8,10 use っさい (いっさい、はっさい、じゅっさい). Question word: 何歳 (なんさい) or おいくつ (more polite). 才 is simplified version of 歳. Note: いくつ can also mean "how old."

## 時

**Reading:** じ
**Meaning:** counter for hours (o'clock)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #counter, #time, #common

### Example Sentences
- 一時 (いちじ) - one o'clock
- 二時 (にじ) - two o'clock
- 三時 (さんじ) - three o'clock
- 何時 (なんじ) - what time

### Notes
Used for: telling time (hours). Irregular: 四時 is よじ (not よんじ), 九時 is くじ (not きゅうじ). Question word: 何時 (なんじ). Always used with です when stating time: 三時です (It's 3 o'clock). Combined with 分 for minutes.

## 日

**Reading:** にち / か
**Meaning:** counter for days, dates
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #counter, #time, #common

### Example Sentences
- 一日 (ついたち/いちにち) - 1st of month / one day
- 二日 (ふつか) - 2nd of month / two days
- 三日 (みっか) - 3rd of month / three days
- 何日 (なんにち) - how many days / what date

### Notes
Used for: dates and duration of days. HIGHLY IRREGULAR for dates 1-10: ついたち、ふつか、みっか、よっか、いつか、むいか、なのか、ようか、ここのか、とおか. After 10: regular じゅういちにち、じゅうににち, etc. For 14,20,24: じゅうよっか、はつか、にじゅうよっか. Duration uses にち: 三日間 (for three days). Question: 何日 (なんにち for duration/date).
