# JLPT N5 Particles

---
## は

**Reading:** wa
**Meaning:** topic marker (indicates the topic of sentence)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #topic-marker, #common

### Example Sentences
- 私は学生です - I am a student (As for me, I'm a student)
- これは本です - This is a book
- 今日は暑いです - Today is hot

### Notes
Pronounced "wa" not "ha." Marks the topic of the sentence (what you're talking about). Different from が which marks the subject. Often used for contrast or to introduce new information. When switching topics, use は. The topic can be implied and omitted in subsequent sentences.

---
## が

**Reading:** ga
**Meaning:** subject marker (indicates the grammatical subject)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #subject-marker, #common

### Example Sentences
- 雨が降ります - Rain falls (It rains)
- 犬がいます - There is a dog
- 誰が来ますか - Who is coming?

### Notes
Marks the grammatical subject performing an action or being described. Used with: 1) existence verbs (いる/ある), 2) potential forms, 3) likes/dislikes (好き/嫌い), 4) abilities (できる), 5) desire expressions (欲しい/たい), 6) question words (誰/何/どこ). Difference from は: が identifies NEW information, は marks KNOWN topics. Example: 象は鼻が長い (As for elephants, their trunks are long) - は for topic (elephants), が for subject (trunks).

## を

**Reading:** o / wo
**Meaning:** direct object marker
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #object-marker, #common

### Example Sentences
- 本を読みます - I read a book
- りんごを食べます - I eat an apple
- 公園を散歩します - I walk through the park

### Notes
Pronounced "o" in modern Japanese, though written as を. Marks the direct object of transitive verbs (the thing being acted upon). Also used with: 1) movement verbs to show the place through which you move (道を歩く - walk along the road), 2) departure points with 出る (家を出る - leave home). Cannot be used with intransitive verbs or adjectives.

---
## に

**Reading:** ni
**Meaning:** to, at, in, on (location/time/direction/indirect object)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #multifunctional, #common

### Example Sentences
- 東京に行きます - I go to Tokyo (direction)
- 部屋に猫がいます - There's a cat in the room (location of existence)
- 友達にあげます - I give it to a friend (indirect object)
- 三時に会います - We meet at 3 o'clock (specific time)

### Notes
Multi-functional particle with main uses: 1) DIRECTION (destination with motion verbs: 行く/来る/帰る), 2) LOCATION (with existence verbs いる/ある and living/sitting verbs 住む/座る), 3) SPECIFIC TIME (時間に、月曜日に), 4) INDIRECT OBJECT (recipient with あげる/もらう/教える), 5) PURPOSE (買い物に行く - go shopping), 6) FREQUENCY (一日に三回 - three times a day). Contrast with で for location of action and へ for direction.

---
## で

**Reading:** de
**Meaning:** at, in, by, with (location of action/means/method)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #multifunctional, #common

### Example Sentences
- 図書館で勉強します - I study at the library (location of action)
- バスで行きます - I go by bus (means of transportation)
- 日本語で話します - I speak in Japanese (means/method)
- 三人で食べます - We eat with three people (group composition)

### Notes
Main uses: 1) LOCATION OF ACTION (where something happens with action verbs, contrast with に which is location of existence), 2) MEANS/METHOD (transportation, tools, language, materials), 3) CAUSE/REASON (地震で壊れた - broke because of earthquake), 4) SCOPE (一番で - number one in/among), 5) TIME LIMIT (一週間で - within one week). Remember: に = static location (existence), で = dynamic location (action).

## へ

**Reading:** e
**Meaning:** to, toward (direction)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #direction, #common

### Example Sentences
- 学校へ行きます - I go to school
- 東へ進みます - I proceed eastward
- 未来へ - Toward the future

### Notes
Pronounced "e" not "he." Indicates direction or destination with motion verbs. Generally interchangeable with に for direction, but へ emphasizes the direction/path while に emphasizes the destination/arrival point. に is more common in everyday speech, へ sounds slightly more formal or poetic. Cannot be used for: specific time, location of existence, indirect object (must use に for these).

## と

**Reading:** to
**Meaning:** and, with, quotation marker
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #multifunctional, #common

### Example Sentences
- 犬と猫 - Dogs and cats (exhaustive listing)
- 友達と行きます - I go with a friend (accompaniment)
- 「はい」と言いました - I said "yes" (quotation)
- りんごはみかんと違います - Apples are different from oranges (comparison)

### Notes
Main uses: 1) EXHAUSTIVE LISTING ("and" for complete lists: AとBとC - contrast with や for partial lists), 2) ACCOMPANIMENT ("with" - doing something together with someone/something), 3) QUOTATION (marks direct/indirect quotes before 言う/思う/聞く), 4) COMPARISON (with 同じ/違う/似ている). For accompaniment, the partner must be specific (友達と OK, but for "with everyone" use みんなで). Cannot use for parallel actions (use -たり-たり instead).

## の

**Reading:** no
**Meaning:** possessive, modification, nominalization
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #modifier, #common

### Example Sentences
- 私の本 - My book (possessive)
- 日本の車 - Japanese car (modification)
- 赤いのがいいです - The red one is good (nominalization)
- 食べるのが好きです - I like eating (nominalization)

### Notes
Main uses: 1) POSSESSION (AのB = B belonging to A or B of A), 2) MODIFICATION (connects two nouns where first modifies second: 学生の田中さん - Tanaka who is a student), 3) NOMINALIZATION (turns verbs/adjectives into noun phrases: 走るのは楽しい - running is fun), 4) APPOSITION (explanation: 先生の山田さん - Mr. Yamada, the teacher), 5) REPLACEMENT (stands in for previously mentioned noun: 大きいの - big one). の can replace が in informal speech in subordinate clauses.

## も

**Reading:** mo
**Meaning:** also, too, even
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #inclusive, #common

### Example Sentences
- 私も学生です - I am also a student
- これもそれも好きです - I like both this and that
- 一つも食べませんでした - I didn't eat even one

### Notes
Replaces は, が, or を to mean "also/too." Use も after other particles (にも, でも, からも) but it replaces は/が/を. When repeated (AもBも), creates patterns: 1) With affirmative = "both A and B", 2) With negative = "neither A nor B". Special patterns: 何も〜ない (nothing at all), どこにも〜ない (nowhere), 誰も〜ない (no one). Emphasizes inclusion or even adds emphasis in negative contexts.

## か

**Reading:** ka
**Meaning:** question marker, or
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- これは本ですか - Is this a book?
- 何を食べますか - What will you eat?
- コーヒーか紅茶 - Coffee or tea
- 誰か来ました - Someone came

### Notes
Main uses: 1) QUESTION MARKER (at sentence end, rising intonation in questions, neutral intonation in polite questions), 2) "OR" (between alternatives: AかB), 3) INDEFINITE (with question words: 何か something, 誰か someone, どこか somewhere - without negative). In casual speech, か is often omitted and replaced with rising intonation only. After だ/です, insert か directly (です → ですか). Question words + か + も + negative = nothing/nowhere/no one (何もない - nothing).

## ね

**Reading:** ne
**Meaning:** sentence-ending particle for confirmation/agreement
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #sentence-ender, #common

### Example Sentences
- いい天気ですね - It's nice weather, isn't it?
- そうですね - That's right / Let me see
- 美味しいですね - It's delicious, isn't it?

### Notes
Sentence-final particle expressing: 1) SEEKING CONFIRMATION/AGREEMENT (like "isn't it?" or "right?"), 2) SHARING FEELINGS (expressing shared experience or emotion), 3) SOFTENING (makes statements less direct/harsh). Similar to English tag questions. Often with rising intonation when seeking agreement, falling when expressing shared feeling. Can be used alone as response (ね〜 = "I know, right"). More gentle than よ. Overuse sounds overly familiar or feminine in some contexts.

## よ

**Reading:** yo
**Meaning:** sentence-ending particle for emphasis/assertion
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #sentence-ender, #common

### Example Sentences
- 明日は休みですよ - Tomorrow is a holiday, you know
- 危ないですよ - It's dangerous! (warning/emphasis)
- 行きますよ - I'm going! (assertion)

### Notes
Sentence-final particle expressing: 1) NEW INFORMATION (telling listener something they don't know), 2) EMPHASIS/ASSERTION (stressing your statement), 3) WARNING/REMINDER (pointing out something important). Falling intonation. More assertive than ね. Can combine with ね (よね) to assert while seeking agreement. Using よ alone can sound pushy if overused, especially to superiors. Common in warnings (気をつけてよ - be careful!) and emphasis (本当ですよ - it's really true!).

## から

**Reading:** kara
**Meaning:** from (starting point), because
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #multifunctional, #common

### Example Sentences
- 駅から歩きます - I walk from the station (starting point - place)
- 九時から始まります - It starts from 9 o'clock (starting point - time)
- 疲れたから休みます - I'll rest because I'm tired (reason)
- 友達から聞きました - I heard from a friend (source)

### Notes
Main uses: 1) STARTING POINT - PLACE (origin or departure point in space), 2) STARTING POINT - TIME (beginning of time period, used with まで for "from...to"), 3) CAUSE/REASON (because - attaches to plain form or です form), 4) SOURCE (from whom you received/heard something). As conjunction meaning "because": Plain form + から or です/だ + から. Can appear mid-sentence or sentence-final. More casual/explanatory than ので. Often paired with まで to show range.

## まで

**Reading:** made
**Meaning:** until, to (ending point)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #limit, #common

### Example Sentences
- 五時まで働きます - I work until 5 o'clock (time limit)
- 駅まで歩きます - I walk to the station (destination/limit)
- 月曜日から金曜日まで - From Monday to Friday
- 子供まで知っています - Even children know (extreme example)

### Notes
Main uses: 1) TIME LIMIT (until a certain time), 2) SPATIAL LIMIT (to/up to a location), 3) EXTENT/DEGREE (even, as far as - emphasizes extremes), 4) RANGE (combined with から: から〜まで = from...to/until). Unlike に which marks arrival, まで marks the endpoint/limit without necessarily implying arrival. Can combine with に (までに) to mean "by" a deadline: 五時までに帰る (return by 5 o'clock - emphasizes the deadline must be met).

## や

**Reading:** ya
**Meaning:** and (non-exhaustive list)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #listing

### Example Sentences
- 犬や猫など - Dogs, cats, and so on
- りんごやバナナを買いました - I bought apples, bananas (and other things)
- 本や雑誌がたくさんあります - There are many books, magazines, etc.

### Notes
Lists items non-exhaustively (partial list with implication of "and other things"). Contrast with と which gives exhaustive/complete lists. や suggests there are more items not mentioned. Usually appears with など (etc.) or other words implying continuation. Cannot be used for: 1) only two items (use と instead), 2) complete sets, 3) actions (use -たり-たり instead). Less common in modern conversation than と, but important for indicating incomplete lists. Similar to "such as" or "like" in English.

## ください

**Reading:** ください
**Meaning:** please (polite request)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #request, #common

### Example Sentences
- 水をください - Please give me water
- 見てください - Please look
- これをください - I'll take this, please
- ちょっと待ってください - Please wait a moment

### Notes
Attached to te-form verbs to make polite requests (verb-て + ください). Can also follow を with noun to request something (noun + を + ください). More polite than command forms but less formal than いただけますか. Very common in daily requests. For even more politeness, add どうぞ before: どうぞ座ってください (please do sit down). Negative requests use ないで: 食べないでください (please don't eat). Essential pattern for N5 level politeness.

## でした

**Reading:** deshita
**Meaning:** was/were (polite past copula)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #copula, #common

### Example Sentences
- 昨日は暑かったです - Yesterday was hot
- 学生でした - I was a student
- いい映画でした - It was a good movie
- 楽しかったです - It was fun

### Notes
Past tense of です. Used with: 1) nouns + でした (本でした - it was a book), 2) na-adjectives + でした (静かでした - it was quiet), 3) i-adjectives + かったです (高かったです - it was expensive). Negative past: じゃありませんでした (wasn't) or じゃなかったです (wasn't - casual). である (plain form) becomes だった in past. Essential for talking about past states and descriptions in polite speech.

## ね (sentence-ending, tag question)

**Reading:** ne
**Meaning:** right? isn't it? (seeking confirmation)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #particle, #confirmation, #common

### Example Sentences
- 寒いですね - It's cold, isn't it?
- 明日ですね - It's tomorrow, right?
- いいですね - That's good, isn't it?
- 行きますね - I'll go, okay?

### Notes
Sentence-final particle for: 1) SEEKING AGREEMENT (wants listener to agree - rising intonation), 2) SOFTENING STATEMENTS (makes declarations less blunt - falling intonation), 3) CHECKING UNDERSTANDING (confirming shared knowledge). English equivalents: "right?", "isn't it?", "don't you think?". Different from よ which asserts new info. When combined (よね), asserts while seeking agreement. Overuse can sound uncertain or overly soft. Essential for natural, interactive Japanese conversation.
