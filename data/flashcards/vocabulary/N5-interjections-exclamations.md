# JLPT N5 Interjections and Exclamations (感動詞)
#
# ABOUT THIS SET:
# Interjections are words/phrases that express emotion, response, or facilitate conversation.
# They include greetings, responses, fillers, apologies, and common set phrases.
# These are essential for natural, polite conversation in Japanese.
#
# CATEGORIES:
# - Greetings and farewells
# - Responses and acknowledgments  
# - Apologies and gratitude
# - Fillers and hesitation words
# - Meal-related phrases
# - Emotional exclamations
#
# Note: Many have specific usage contexts (formal/casual, timing, etc.)

---
## はい

**Reading:** はい
**Meaning:** yes
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- 学生ですか。はい、学生です - Are you a student? Yes, I am
- はい、分かりました - Yes, I understood
- はい、どうぞ - Yes, here you are

### Notes
Affirmative response. More polite than うん (casual yes). Also used to: 1) acknowledge you're listening, 2) answer when called, 3) show agreement. Can be repeated (はい、はい) to show active listening, but overuse can sound impatient.

## いいえ

**Reading:** いいえ
**Meaning:** no
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- 学生ですか。いいえ、学生じゃありません - Are you a student? No, I'm not
- いいえ、大丈夫です - No, it's okay
- いいえ、違います - No, that's wrong

### Notes
Negative response. More polite than ううん (casual no). Japanese often avoid direct "no" and use softer expressions instead. いいえ、結構です (no, I'm fine/I'll pass) is a polite refusal. Sometimes shortened to いえ in conversation.

## ありがとう

**Reading:** ありがとう
**Meaning:** thank you
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #gratitude, #common

### Example Sentences
- ありがとう - Thank you
- ありがとうございます - Thank you (polite)
- どうもありがとうございます - Thank you very much

### Notes
Expression of gratitude. ありがとう = casual. ありがとうございます = polite. どうもありがとうございます = very polite/emphatic. Past tense (ありがとうございました) for completed favors. Essential phrase for daily interaction.

## すみません

**Reading:** すみません
**Meaning:** excuse me, I'm sorry
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #apology, #common

### Example Sentences
- すみません、ちょっといいですか - Excuse me, do you have a moment?
- すみません - I'm sorry / Excuse me
- すみません、駅はどこですか - Excuse me, where is the station?

### Notes
Multi-purpose: 1) apologize, 2) get attention, 3) thank (for trouble caused). More casual than 申し訳ありません. Often shortened to すいません in casual speech. Very common and versatile expression.

## ごめんなさい

**Reading:** ごめんなさい
**Meaning:** I'm sorry
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #apology, #common

### Example Sentences
- ごめんなさい、遅れました - I'm sorry I'm late
- 本当にごめんなさい - I'm really sorry
- ごめん - Sorry (very casual)

### Notes
Apology. More personal/sincere than すみません. ごめん = very casual (to friends/family only). Use すみません for strangers or less serious situations. ごめんなさい for genuine apologies.

## こんにちは

**Reading:** こんにちは
**Meaning:** hello, good afternoon
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #greeting, #common

### Example Sentences
- こんにちは - Hello
- こんにちは、元気ですか - Hello, how are you?
- 先生、こんにちは - Hello, teacher

### Notes
Greeting used during daytime (roughly 10am-6pm). Literally "as for today." Written with は but read "wa." Most common general greeting. Not used with family members at home.

## おはよう

**Meaning:** good morning
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #greeting, #common

### Example Sentences
- おはよう - Good morning (casual)

## さようなら

**Reading:** さようなら
**Meaning:** goodbye
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #farewell, #common

### Example Sentences
- さようなら - Goodbye
- さようなら、また明日 - Goodbye, see you tomorrow
- 先生、さようなら - Goodbye, teacher

### Notes
Formal goodbye, often implies won't see person for a while or may not see again. Casual alternatives: じゃあね、バイバイ. To people you'll see soon: また (see you), また明日 (see you tomorrow). さようなら sounds final.

## おやすみ

**Reading:** おやすみ
**Meaning:** good night
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #farewell, #common

### Example Sentences
- おやすみ - Good night (casual)

## いってきます

**Reading:** いってきます
**Meaning:** I'm leaving, I'll be back
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #departure, #common

### Example Sentences
- いってきます - I'm leaving (said when leaving home)
- 学校に行ってきます - I'm going to school (and will come back)

### Notes
Said when leaving home/office (with intention to return). Literally "I'll go and come back." Response: いってらっしゃい. Unique Japanese custom showing temporary departure. Very common daily phrase.

## いってらっしゃい

**Reading:** いってらっしゃい
**Meaning:** have a good day, see you later
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #farewell, #common

### Example Sentences
- いってらっしゃい - Have a good day (response to いってきます)
- 気をつけて、いってらっしゃい - Take care, see you later

### Notes
Response to いってきます. Said by person staying behind to person leaving (with intention to return). Shows care and expectation of return. Casual form: いってらっしゃい. Very polite: いってらっしゃいませ.

## ただいま

**Reading:** ただいま
**Meaning:** I'm home, I'm back
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #arrival, #common

### Example Sentences
- ただいま - I'm home

## いただきます

**Reading:** いただきます
**Meaning:** thank you for the meal (before eating)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #meal, #common

### Example Sentences
- いただきます - (said before eating)
- では、いただきます - Well then, let's eat

### Notes
Said before eating. Expresses gratitude to those who prepared the meal and to the food itself. Literally "I humbly receive." Hands often placed together in prayer position. Important cultural custom, always say before eating.

## ごちそうさまでした

**Reading:** ごちそうさまでした
**Meaning:** thank you for the meal (after eating)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #meal, #common

### Example Sentences
- ごちそうさまでした - Thank you for the meal (said after eating)
- 美味しかったです。ごちそうさまでした - It was delicious. Thank you for the meal

### Notes
Said after finishing a meal. Expresses gratitude for the meal. Literally "it was a feast." Casual form: ごちそうさま. Important cultural custom, always say after eating. Shows appreciation to cook/host.

## あのう / あの

**Reading:** あのう / あの
**Meaning:** um, excuse me (filler)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #filler, #common

### Example Sentences
- あのう、すみません - Um, excuse me
- あの、ちょっといいですか - Um, do you have a moment?
- あのう、それは... - Um, that is...

### Notes
Hesitation word/filler. Used to: 1) get attention politely, 2) hesitate before speaking, 3) soften what you're about to say. あのう is more polite/careful than あの. Very common in natural speech. Shows politeness and consideration.

## ええと

**Reading:** ええと
**Meaning:** let me see, well (filler)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #filler, #common

### Example Sentences
- ええと、名前は... - Let me see, the name is...
- ええと、どこだっけ - Well, where was it?
- ええと、そうですね - Let's see, yes

### Notes
Filler word while thinking/remembering. Shows you're processing or trying to recall information. Casual equivalent: えーと. Natural in conversation, shows thinking process. Similar to "um" or "let's see" in English.

## そうですね

**Reading:** そうですね
**Meaning:** let me see, that's right
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- そうですね - That's right / Let me see
- そうですね、いい考えですね - Yes, that's a good idea
- そうですね、明日がいいです - Let's see, tomorrow is good

### Notes
Response phrase. Two uses: 1) agreement ("that's right"), 2) thinking/considering ("let me see"). With rising intonation = considering. With falling intonation = agreeing. Very common in conversation. Casual: そうだね.

## そうですか

**Reading:** そうですか
**Meaning:** I see, is that so
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- 明日休みです。そうですか - Tomorrow is a holiday. I see
- そうですか、知りませんでした - Is that so, I didn't know
- そうですか - I see

### Notes
Acknowledgment phrase. Shows you've received/understood information. Not agreement, just acknowledgment. Casual: そうか、そっか. Can sound cold if overused. Rising intonation shows interest, falling can sound indifferent.

## 本当ですか

**Reading:** ほんとうですか
**Meaning:** really? is that true?
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #response, #common

### Example Sentences
- 彼が来ます。本当ですか - He's coming. Really?
- 本当ですか、知りませんでした - Really? I didn't know
- 本当? - Really? (casual)

### Notes
Expresses surprise or asks for confirmation. Shows interest or disbelief. Casual: 本当? (ほんと?), マジで? (very casual). Rising intonation. Common in conversation when hearing unexpected news.

## えっ / え

**Reading:** えっ / え
**Meaning:** huh? what? (surprise/didn't hear)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #interjection, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- えっ、本当ですか - What? Really?
- え、何ですか - Huh? What is it?
- えっ? - What? (didn't hear)

### Notes
Shows surprise or requests repetition. えっ = sharper surprise. え = gentler. Can be rude if used too directly with superiors. In polite situations, use すみません、もう一度お願いします (sorry, once more please).
