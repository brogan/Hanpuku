# JLPT N5 Numbers (数字)
#
# ABOUT THIS SET:
# Japanese has two number systems: native Japanese (ひとつ、ふたつ...) and Sino-Japanese (いち、に、さん...).
# The Sino-Japanese system is more commonly used and extends infinitely.
# Native Japanese numbers typically only go up to 10.
#
# READING PATTERNS:
# Numbers 1-10 have multiple readings depending on what they're counting.
# Large numbers: 100 (ひゃく), 1000 (せん), 10000 (まん), 100000000 (おく)
#
# Note: Numbers combine with counters, which may cause phonetic changes (see Counter set).

---
## 零 / ゼロ

**Reading:** れい / ゼロ
**Meaning:** zero
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #common

### Example Sentences
- 零度 (れいど) - zero degrees
- ゼロから始める - Start from zero
- 電話番号はゼロ三... - The phone number is 03...

### Notes
Two ways to say zero: 零 (れい - more formal), ゼロ (katakana, common in daily use). ゼロ is often used for phone numbers and temperatures. れい is used in more formal/mathematical contexts.

## 一

**Reading:** いち / ひと(つ)
**Meaning:** one
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #common

### Example Sentences
- 一つ (ひとつ) - one (thing)
- 一人 (ひとり) - one person
- 一時 (いちじ) - one o'clock
- 一日 (ついたち / いちにち) - 1st of month / one day

### Notes
Multiple readings: いち (Sino-Japanese - most common), ひと (native - in compounds). Changes to いっ before certain sounds: 一本 (いっぽん), 一回 (いっかい), 一匹 (いっぴき). As date: ついたち (1st). Very important to learn different readings with different counters.

## 四

**Reading:** し / よん / よ(っつ)
**Meaning:** four
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #common

### Example Sentences
- 四つ (よっつ) - four (things)
- 四人 (よにん) - four people
- 四時 (よじ) - four o'clock
- 四日 (よっか) - 4th of month / four days

### Notes
Multiple readings: し, よん, よ. し is avoided in many contexts (sounds like 死 - death). よん is most common. よ appears in dates and native counting. As time: よじ (4 o'clock). As date: よっか (4th). Irregular: 四つ (よっつ), 四人 (よにん - not よんにん).

## 百

**Reading:** ひゃく / びゃく / ぴゃく
**Meaning:** hundred
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #common

### Example Sentences
- 百円 (ひゃくえん) - 100 yen
- 三百 (さんびゃく) - 300
- 六百 (ろっぴゃく) - 600
- 八百 (はっぴゃく) - 800

### Notes
Phonetic changes: Regular ひゃく for 100, 200, 400, 500, 700, 900. Changes to びゃく for 300 (さんびゃく). Changes to ぴゃく for 600 (ろっぴゃく), 800 (はっぴゃく). Essential for prices and large numbers.

## 千

**Reading:** せん / ぜん
**Meaning:** thousand
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #common

### Example Sentences
- 千円 (せんえん) - 1,000 yen
- 三千 (さんぜん) - 3,000
- 八千 (はっせん) - 8,000
- 一万 (いちまん) - 10,000

### Notes
Phonetic changes: Regular せん for 1000, 2000, 4000, 5000, 6000, 7000, 9000. Changes to ぜん for 3000 (さんぜん). 8000 is はっせん (gemination). Essential for larger prices and numbers.

## 万

**Reading:** まん
**Meaning:** ten thousand
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #common

### Example Sentences
- 一万円 (いちまんえん) - 10,000 yen
- 十万 (じゅうまん) - 100,000
- 百万 (ひゃくまん) - 1,000,000

### Notes
Important: Japanese groups numbers by 10,000 (万), not 1,000 like English. 10,000 = 一万 (not 十千). No phonetic changes. Essential for understanding Japanese number system and prices. 100,000 = 十万, 1,000,000 = 百万.

## 何

**Reading:** なん / なに
**Meaning:** what, how many
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- 何人 (なんにん) - how many people
- 何時 (なんじ) - what time
- 何本 (なんぼん) - how many (long objects)
- 何個 (なんこ) - how many (small objects)

### Notes
Used before counters to ask "how many." Read as なん before counters: 何時 (なんじ), 何人 (なんにん), 何本 (なんぼん), 何歳 (なんさい). Essential question word for quantities. Alternative: いくつ (how many) for general counting.

## 幾つ

**Reading:** いくつ
**Meaning:** how many, how old
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- いくつですか - How many? / How old are you?
- いくつ買いますか - How many will you buy?
- いくつありますか - How many are there?

### Notes
Question word for quantity using native Japanese counting. Can mean "how many" or "how old" depending on context. More casual than 何. Often used when asking children their age. For general objects without specific counters.

## 半分

**Reading:** はんぶん
**Meaning:** half
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number

### Example Sentences
- 半分ください - Please give me half
- リンゴの半分 - Half of the apple
- 半分食べました - I ate half

### Notes
Means "half" or "50%." Also 半 (はん) alone for half: 三時半 (3:30 - half past three). 半分 emphasizes the portion/fraction. Very common in daily conversation for dividing things.

## 全部

**Reading:** ぜんぶ
**Meaning:** all, everything, whole
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #number, #common

### Example Sentences
- 全部食べました - I ate everything
- 全部でいくらですか - How much is it all together?
- 全部好きです - I like all of it

### Notes
Means "all" or "everything" (quantity). Different from みんな (everyone - people). 全部で = "in total/altogether." Very common for emphasizing completeness or totality.
