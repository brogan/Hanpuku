# JLPT N5 Pronouns (代名詞)
#
# ABOUT THIS SET:
# Japanese pronouns are organized into demonstrative systems and personal pronouns.
# The demonstrative system uses ko-so-a-do pattern based on distance from speaker/listener.
# Personal pronouns vary by formality, gender, and social context.
#
# DEMONSTRATIVE PATTERN:
# これ/この (ko-) = near speaker
# それ/その (so-) = near listener  
# あれ/あの (a-) = far from both
# どれ/どの (do-) = question word
#
# Note: Pronouns are often omitted in Japanese when context is clear.

---
## 私

**Reading:** わたし / わたくし
**Meaning:** I, me (neutral/polite)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #personal, #common

### Example Sentences
- 私は学生です - I am a student
- 私の本 - My book
- 私に聞いてください - Please ask me

### Notes
Most common and neutral first-person pronoun. Safe in all contexts. わたくし is more formal. Can be used by anyone regardless of gender. Often omitted when context is clear. Possessive: 私の (watashi no - my/mine).

---
## 僕

**Reading:** ぼく
**Meaning:** I, me (casual, male)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #personal, #common

### Example Sentences
- 僕は学生です - I am a student
- 僕の本 - My book
- 僕も行きます - I'll go too

### Notes
Casual first-person pronoun primarily used by males. Less formal than 私. Common among boys and young men. Can be used by males in casual/friendly settings. Not appropriate for very formal situations. Possessive: 僕の (boku no - my/mine).

---
## あなた

**Reading:** あなた
**Meaning:** you
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #personal, #common

### Example Sentences
- あなたは学生ですか - Are you a student?
- あなたの本 - Your book
- あなたが好きです - I love you

### Notes
Second-person pronoun, but USE SPARINGLY. Japanese prefer using names or titles instead (田中さん、先生). Can sound direct/rude in many contexts. Acceptable when: 1) addressing strangers, 2) in questions, 3) romantic contexts (wives to husbands). Often better to omit or use person's name. Possessive: あなたの (anata no - your/yours).

## 彼

**Reading:** かれ
**Meaning:** he, him, boyfriend
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #personal, #common

### Example Sentences
- 彼は学生です - He is a student
- 彼の本 - His book
- 彼は私の友達です - He is my friend

### Notes
Third-person pronoun for males. Can mean "he" or "boyfriend" depending on context. Less commonly used than English "he" - Japanese often uses names or titles. Possessive: 彼の (kare no - his). As "boyfriend": 彼ができました (I got a boyfriend).

## これ

**Reading:** これ
**Meaning:** this (thing near speaker)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #common

### Example Sentences
- これは本です - This is a book
- これをください - Please give me this
- これが好きです - I like this

### Notes
Demonstrative pronoun (ko-series) for things near speaker. Used independently as noun. For modifying nouns, use この instead. Can point to physical objects or abstract concepts just mentioned. Question form: どれ (which one).

---
## それ

**Reading:** それ
**Meaning:** that (thing near listener)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #common

### Example Sentences
- それは本です - That is a book
- それをください - Please give me that
- それはいいですね - That's good

### Notes
Demonstrative pronoun (so-series) for things near listener or previously mentioned. Used independently as noun. For modifying nouns, use その instead. Often refers to things listener has or things just mentioned in conversation.

---
## あれ

**Reading:** あれ
**Meaning:** that (thing over there, far from both)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #common

### Example Sentences
- あれは何ですか - What is that (over there)?
- あれを見てください - Please look at that
- あれが富士山です - That's Mt. Fuji

### Notes
Demonstrative pronoun (a-series) for things far from both speaker and listener. Used independently as noun. For modifying nouns, use あの instead. Points to distant objects or well-known things both parties can see.

---
## どれ

**Reading:** どれ
**Meaning:** which (thing)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- どれが好きですか - Which one do you like?
- どれを買いますか - Which will you buy?
- どれでもいいです - Any one is fine

### Notes
Demonstrative question pronoun (do-series). Asks "which" among three or more options. For two options, use どっち/どちら. Used independently. For modifying nouns, use どの instead. With でも becomes "any/whichever": どれでも.

---
## この

**Reading:** この
**Meaning:** this (modifier near speaker)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #common

### Example Sentences
- この本 - This book
- この人は誰ですか - Who is this person?
- この店が好きです - I like this store

### Notes
Demonstrative modifier (ko-series). Always modifies a noun (cannot stand alone). For standalone, use これ. Points to things near speaker. Question form: どの (which).

---
## その

**Reading:** その
**Meaning:** that (modifier near listener)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #common

### Example Sentences
- その本 - That book
- その人は誰ですか - Who is that person?
- その店が好きです - I like that store

### Notes
Demonstrative modifier (so-series). Always modifies a noun (cannot stand alone). For standalone, use それ. Points to things near listener or previously mentioned topics.

---
## あの

**Reading:** あの
**Meaning:** that (modifier, far from both)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #common

### Example Sentences
- あの本 - That book (over there)
- あの人は誰ですか - Who is that person?
- あの山が富士山です - That mountain is Mt. Fuji

### Notes
Demonstrative modifier (a-series). Always modifies a noun (cannot stand alone). For standalone, use あれ. Points to things far from both speaker and listener, or well-known shared references.

---
## どの

**Reading:** どの
**Meaning:** which (modifier)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- どの本が好きですか - Which book do you like?
- どの人ですか - Which person is it?
- どの店ですか - Which store?

### Notes
Demonstrative question modifier (do-series). Always modifies a noun (cannot stand alone). For standalone, use どれ. Asks "which" among multiple options. With でも becomes "any/whichever": どの本でも (any book).

## ここ

**Reading:** ここ
**Meaning:** here (place near speaker)
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #location, #common

### Example Sentences
- ここは学校です - This place is a school
- ここに来てください - Please come here
- ここで待ちます - I'll wait here

### Notes
Demonstrative location word (ko-series). Indicates place near speaker. With に: ここに (to here, at here). With で: ここで (at here - doing action). With から: ここから (from here). Question form: どこ (where).

## どこ

**Reading:** どこ
**Meaning:** where
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #demonstrative, #location, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- どこに行きますか - Where will you go?
- どこで買いましたか - Where did you buy it?
- どこから来ましたか - Where did you come from?

### Notes
Demonstrative question location word (do-series). With に: どこに (to where). With で: どこで (at where - doing action). With から: どこから (from where). With でも: どこでも (anywhere). Essential question word.

---
## 誰

**Reading:** だれ / どなた
**Meaning:** who
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- 誰ですか - Who is it?
- 誰が来ますか - Who is coming?
- 誰の本ですか - Whose book is it?

### Notes
Question pronoun for people. だれ is casual, どなた is polite. With が: 誰が (who - subject). With の: 誰の (whose). With も + negative: 誰も〜ない (no one). With か: 誰か (someone). With でも: 誰でも (anyone).

---
## 何

**Reading:** なに / なん
**Meaning:** what
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #question, #common

### Example Sentences
- これは何ですか - What is this?
- 何を食べますか - What will you eat?
- 何時ですか - What time is it?

### Notes
Question pronoun for things. なに before particles, なん before counters and です. With が: 何が (what - subject). With の: 何の (what kind of). With も + negative: 何も〜ない (nothing). With か: 何か (something). With でも: 何でも (anything). Before counters: 何人、何時、何本, etc.

---
## 自分

**Reading:** じぶん
**Meaning:** oneself, myself
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #reflexive

### Example Sentences
- 自分で作りました - I made it myself
- 自分の部屋 - My own room
- 自分のことは自分でします - I do my own things myself

### Notes
Reflexive pronoun meaning "oneself." Can refer to any person depending on context (myself, yourself, himself, etc.). Often emphasizes doing something personally: 自分で (by oneself). Possessive: 自分の (one's own). More emphatic than just using 私.

---
## みんな

**Reading:** みんな
**Meaning:** everyone, all
**Type:** vocabulary
**Level:** N5
**Tags:** #jlpt-n5, #pronoun, #common

### Example Sentences
- みんな来ました - Everyone came
- みんなで食べましょう - Let's all eat together
- みんなの本 - Everyone's books

### Notes
Collective pronoun meaning "everyone" or "all people." With で: みんなで (all together, as a group). With の: みんなの (everyone's). Common in daily conversation. Unlike 全員 which is more formal and means "all members."
